"The Cartographers Guilds struck a Map of the Empire whose size was that of the Empire, and which coincided point for point with it. The following Generations, who were not so fond of the Study of Cartography as their Forebears had been, saw that that vast map was Useless, and not without some Pitilessness was it, that they delivered it up to the Inclemencies of Sun and Winters. In the Deserts of the West, still today, there are Tattered Ruins of that Map."

– Jorge Luis Borges, "On Exactitude in Science" (1946)

SIMULCAST

Los Angeles Ends
(hypertext LP, 2019)

Simulcast is a multimedia art project driven by Los Angeles tenant Tristan Friedberg Rodman.

Focused by grief, climate change, and critical histories of their city, Simulcast's work cuts from net art, creative nonfiction, and indie rock.

It sprawls, fractured and interconnected, like Los Angeles itself.
